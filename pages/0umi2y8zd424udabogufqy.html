Curriculum Highlights

Mathematics

Math Curriculum

We have Unit 3 Multiplication and Division of Fractions

Students will be working on the following:


  • Algebraic thinking with fractions
  • Multiplying with Non-Unit Fractions
  • Multiply a Fraction by a Fraction
  • Review Addition of Fractions
  • Solving Division Problems
  • Distinguish Multiplication from Division
  • Review Operations with Fractions



Math Facts Tests

Students will be tested on their math facts on (Wednesday- optional and) Friday each week. Once they pass all of their multiplication tables they will work on their division tables. When students have passed all the the 1-12 tests he or she will be permitted to play math games during this time.


Xtra Math- All students have logins and passwords to practice their math facts online.

Reading Workshop

Reading Logs

Students should be reading 30 min. per night. February Reading Logs are due Thursday, March 1st. Any in class reading assignments done as homework may count toward their Reading Log.


Class Reading - Check out Google Classroom

Our class is reading HOOT by Carl Hiaasen. This Newbery Honor-winner, involves new kids, dealing with bullies, eco-awareness and endangered owls. Students will be reading in class and at home.


Each student has his or her own copy of Hoot. Chapters 9-11 will be discussed this week in class. Students have the option of blogging most of their reading activities on Google Classroom! Both blogging and hard copy assignments in their Reading Journals will be accepted.


Students will be expected to read a required amount of pages as determined in class. We will be reading as much as we can together. For discussion purposes students may need to do some of the assignments and reading at home. You should be seeing Hoot come home at least three days a week.

Writer's Workshop

Persuasive Essay Unit - Check out Google Classroom for more info.

This week we will be wrapping up our Persuasive Writing Unit. Students have been immersed in the research of video games and the implications of their use. "Should kids play video games?" Students who do not complete their writing in class will be asked to continue their writing at home and during study hall.

Spelling and Vocabulary

Students have assigned words and Tic-Tac-Toe Spelling Activities. Students must choose three activities in a row of three to complete every two weeks. A vocabulary quiz may be given at the end of the two week period. Sudents are expected to understand the meaning of the words on their assigned list.

Addams InnovateX Businesses

Students have created their company teams. One of the company's requirements is to create a product or service. Companies will receive bonus points for using recycled materials in their product. They will be using mathematics and design principles to create this reproducible item of their choice. We are planning for each team/business to have the opportunity to sell these to other members of the 5th grade for tickets during an open market day. This week students will continue to work on their business plan and should begin to consider a product their team can make that may be popular among their peers.

Second Grade Buddies

Our 5th graders love assisting their 2nd grade buddies in Ms. Morrell's class with math, reading and technology! As our buddies learn new typing skills our 5th graders are being asked to help them edit their writing.
