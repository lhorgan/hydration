Scottsbluff YMCA host Health Awareness Day

By Brian Sherrod | 
Posted: Wed 3:10 PM, Jan 10, 2018

SCOTTSBLUFF, Neb. Many people say that their New Year’s resolution is to get themselves fit and healthy so one local facility is helping to make that happen.

Scottsbluff Family YMCA hosted a Health Awareness Day to give members of the community tips on how to give their body the healthy nutrition it deserves while maintaining a steady workout which will decrease the sedentary trend.

“I just believe that we sit a lot,” explained Terri Allen, Community Health Representative. “Our young people and even older people, we have computers and we’re not as active as we use to be and so I think we need to push ourselves away from our chairs and try to be a little more active.”

Along with the tips, members of the public received blood pressure tests and take home health packets to get their healthy journey started.

“Once you take that first step, then it’s easier to take the second step, then the third step and before you know it, you made a little bit of lead way in whatever health change that you want to work on,” said Allen.

Allen also mentions that the Community Health Center offers free classes and programs including Tai Chi, Fall Prevention, National Diabetes Prevention, Health Coaching Classes for Women and Colon Cancer Kits.

“Taking classes, coming to the Y or even just walking will make a huge difference in your lifestyle choice,” explained Allen. “Walking is one of the easiest things that you can do and our health is one of the most important things that we have control over so we can make that choice to get better so that we can be healthy as we grow older.”
