Event Details

Versed: Memento Mori

Death and our understanding of death are absolutely empty. No clear image comes to mind. The concept of death has a use for the living. All we can say about death is that it is either real or it is not real. If it is real, then the end of one's life is a simple end. If it is not real, then the end of one's embodied life is not true death, but transference to another life. We are all equals when thinking about death, lost in the ignorance of our own limitations, death is a fact of life but a mystery of creation.

 

Cost: $40 for the series. Dinner provided.

Location: Cathedral Center 840 Echo Park Ave. Los Angeles CA 90026. Join us from 7:30pm-9:30pm on Tuesday evenings. Dinner provided.

 

 

Tuesday, February 27th --

To Be Forgotten – We are all forgotten. What does it mean for our life’s work to be left behind, unacknowledged by history? Is legacy an important aspect of life? Does an unfounded belief in the importance of legacy stop us from living into the fullness of our true selves?

 

Tuesday, March 6 –

Death Wrapped in Creation – Death comes for other people, but never for me. We know that we are going to die, but as long as we keep living, death remains over the horizon—a future end. We know we will die. How does an expiration date augment how we live? Do we accept it? Is death a required piece of creation?

 

Tuesday, March 13 -

Fear, Grief and Loss with the Dead and Dying – CS Lewis compares the healing from loss to that of an amputated leg; there is so much healing beyond just the closing of the wound. When numbness and loss derails everything; when you scream at the universe, “Where is God?” When death overturns life, where is the light? 

 

Tuesday, March 20 – 

Cheapness of Life -- We may be able to face other people’s death but may find it virtually impossible to come to terms with our own death. Are we sensitive to death or just to personal loss? Why are we not effected when the dead and dying are the other, the unknown, or the distant?
