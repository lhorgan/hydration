Mike Zimmer on hot streak coming off of extended breaks

ByAnthony Broome Jan 8, 2018
0 Comments
  • Edit
  • Slot to 247Sports Home
  • Slot to Minnesota Vikings Newsletter

Mike Zimmer has had some downtime with his team to get healthy and reload heading into the NFL playoffs with his Minnesota Vikings having a first-round bye to the Divisional Round.

History shows that may be a positive thing for this team heading into the showdown with the New Orleans Saints.

In the four seasons as head coach, Zimmer and the Vikings are 8-4 in games where they have had more than eight days to prepare for an opponent. Lost in that is an even more impressive stat of 7-1 in their most recent games of that variety and a current four-game win streak.

Here's the breakdown:

2014 (1-2 overall): 34-6 win at St. Louis Rams (Week 1), 17-3 loss vs. Detroit Lions (Week 6), 21-13 loss at Chicago Bears (Week 11)

2015 (2-1 overall): 20-3 loss at San Francisco 49ers (Week 1), 16-10 win vs. Kansas City Chiefs (Week 6), 38-17 win vs. Chicago Bears (Week 15)

2016 (2-1 overall): 25-16 win at Tennessee Titans (Week 1), 21-10 loss at Philadelphia Eagles (Week 7), 25-16 win at Jacksonville Jaguars (Week 14)

2017: (3-0 overall): 29-19 win vs. New Orleans Saints (Week 1), 38-30 win at Washington Redskins (Week 10), 14-9 win at Atlanta Falcons (Week 13)

(The Vikings will welcome the Saints to U.S. Bank Stadium for an NFC Divisional Round showdown this week --Don't miss out on the action. Sign up for our FREE newsletter today!)

The numbers could be coincidental here, but the recent resume does not lie and it shows that this is one of the better prepared teams in the league, especially considering that it has been a span of three different starting quarterbacks playing in that stretch of time.

It will be a tougher task this weekend with the Saints and Vikings having met once this year already and both squads looking different in a variety of ways, but those worried that the extra few days off could affect the team may feel a bit better now.
