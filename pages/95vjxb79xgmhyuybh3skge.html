A popular candle store is leaving Houston's Rice Village after another busy holiday season.

A Yankee Candle rep told Chron.com Tuesday that it is closing its shop in Rice Village at the end of the month.

RELATED: Video: Texas mom claims she was burnt blowing out a candle

This is the only Houston-area location said to be closing. There was an online rumor that the chain was pulling out of the area altogether, but Yankee Candle assures Chron.com that that is not the case.

There are six other locations in the Houston region for shoppers to choose from. Houstonians only have a few more weeks to stock up on the popular candles at rock bottom prices in Rice Village though.

There are currently Yankee Candle shops in malls across all 50 states in the country. Founded in 1969, the company is headquartered in South Deerfield, Massachusetts.

Craig Hlavaty is a reporter for Chron.com and HoustonChronicle.com. He's an intolerable native Texan with too much ink in his skin and too much brisket stuck in his teeth.
